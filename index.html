Escape from Alcatraz — June 11, 1962

Three inmates escaped from Alcatraz Federal Penitentiary in San Francisco Bay. Using handmade tools, they dug through walls and created dummy heads to fool guards. They were never found, and their fate remains a mystery.

The Great Blizzard — March 11–14, 1888

A devastating snowstorm hit the northeastern United States, burying towns under feet of snow. Thousands were stranded, trains stalled, and hundreds lost their lives. It remains one of the deadliest weather disasters in U.S. history.

The Great Molasses Flood — January 15, 1919

A massive tank of molasses in Boston burst, sending a wave over the streets. 21 people died, many were injured, and the sticky aftermath lingered for months.
